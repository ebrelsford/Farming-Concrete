Farming Concrete is a volunteer, citizen science project to measure how much food is grown in New York City's community gardens. Calculating the area, weight, and monetary value of food grown in these spaces highlights the value of community gardens as important green spaces run by and for neighborhood residents and gives them a defined place in the context of the urban food system.

A team of more than twenty volunteer researchers, students, interns, and gardeners are working with hundreds of gardens to map areas under production, measure total acreage, and track harvest volume by crop. This map will continue to change and develop as we continue our work for the duration of 2010. In the end, the data will be able to answer for each garden and for all gardens as a whole:

"We at ___ Community Garden grew ___ pounds of food in ____ square feet, which is worth $$___, served approximately ___ people, and prevented approximately __ pounds of greenhouse gases from entering the atmosphere."

Farming Concrete is fiscally sponsored by the Open Space Institute, Inc. as part of their Citizen Action Program. More than 100 gardeners have been weighing their yield by crop, recording the number of plants they have as well. Meanwhile, gardeners and Farming Concrete volunteers and interns have been conducting crop inventory to be able to include the vegetable beds of those gardeners who are not weighing their produce. Here's how this worked:

Data collection

  • Contact gardeners on foot or over the phone

    • If garden was interested in participating:

    • Bring a scale and a harvest log
    • Map the garden (areas under production, crop inventory)

    For the rest of the growing season:

    • Gardeners weigh their produce
    • Outreach and mapping continues

Data analysis

  • Calculate total yield recorded so far for each plant of each crop recorded by each gardener
  • Calculate average yield of 1 plant of each crop recorded so far (July & August)
  • Tally number of plants of each crop for each garden
  • Apply average yield for each crop to number of plants tallied for each garden

In other words

Weighing = pounds per plant of each crop
Mapping = number of plants of each crop per garden
Yield = pounds of each crop per garden
Many thanks to our partners, GreenThumb (NYC Parks & Recreation), Just Food, and New York Restoration Project, for their time, support, and resources - they have been critical to making this project a success. The New School has also acted generously in providing us with a humbling amount of student help as well as space at their exhibit this fall, Living Concrete/Carrot City.

We are very grateful for two studies that took place in the past few years, measuring food production in community gardens in Boston (Boston Natural Areas Network 2009) and in Philadelphia (UPenn 2009). We owe many aspects of our methodology to them.

We also want to thank Megan Gregory, PhD candidate at Cornell University, for sharing her work with us. She has and continues to offer critical guidance in methodology and implementation.
  1. Why can't I click on the gardens? In keeping gardeners' privacy in mind, we've kept this map anonymous. We are enormously grateful for their help and participation and would not want to show more than what is necessary to make this tool useful. If you are a gardener at one of the participating gardens and would like your garden's name, address, etc. to be visible on the map, please contact eric (at) farmingconcrete (dot) org.
  2. Why are only these gardens on the map? These are the gardens we were able to get to and map so far this season. We're still working hard to include all of the gardens that would like to be included so that we can get a fuller picture of food production in NYC community gardens.
  3. How can I get my garden on the map? If you're a community gardener who would like to be on the map and receive a personalized estimate of food yield for your garden, please contact gardens (at) farmingconcrete (dot) org.
  4. How can I volunteer for this project? To help us conduct crop inventory and make this map more complete, please contact volunteer (at) farmingconcrete (dot) org.
  5. When will this be done/complete? 2012 will be our last official season.
